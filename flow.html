Epilepsy Market Potential

Flow Neuroscience’s tDCS headset, CE-marked for depression since 2019, serves over 400 NHS patients and is expanding to 15 UK trusts as of October 2024 [Guardian]. No epilepsy-specific trials exist as of April 2025, limiting direct market entry into the $551.1M epilepsy device market [Credence Research]. The 33% comorbidity rate (16.5M patients) offers a crossover opportunity, but competition from VNS/DBS and regulatory barriers pose challenges [Epilepsy Foundation]. Flow could capture 5–10% of the market by 2030 with $20–$30M in epilepsy trial funding.

Technology Details

Flow’s tDCS headset delivers low-intensity currents to the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex, approved for home use in UK/EU [Flow Neuroscience]. It’s under FDA review for depression, with trials showing efficacy over antidepressants [Medscape].

Key Features:

  • • Non-invasive tDCS, 400x weaker than ECT
  • • Bluetooth app with telemedicine support
  • • 30-minute sessions, 5–6 weeks
  • • Over 14,000 depression users (2024, estimated)
